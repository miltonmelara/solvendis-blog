## The Invisible Shift

For two years, most companies treated AI like R&D:

- Small teams testing prompts
- Isolated use cases
- “Let’s see what this can do” budgets

That phase is over.

When an enterprise AI company hits $240M ARR with 50% quarter-over-quarter growth, it means procurement signed off. Security approved it. Finance modeled it. Leadership committed to it.

AI is no longer being tried.

It’s being embedded.

## What This Actually Changes

When AI becomes a line item instead of a lab experiment, the math changes.

**Margin math changes.**

If your competitor automates 30% of delivery and you don’t, their cost structure compounds while yours stays linear.

**Vendor selection becomes strategic.**

Model efficiency isn’t a technical detail - it’s cost control. GPU efficiency becomes a differentiator. Infrastructure decisions ripple into gross margin.

**Internal agents replace manual workflows.**

Secure, enterprise-grade AI agents are being built into systems - not sitting in browser tabs.

The workflow shifts from:

Human -> Task -> Human review

to:

Human judgment -> Machine execution -> Human oversight

That’s leverage.
