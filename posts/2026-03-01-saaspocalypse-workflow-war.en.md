# The SaaSpocalypse Is Really a Workflow War

This week, TechCrunch described what some investors are calling the “SaaSpocalypse.”

Public SaaS stocks are sliding. Per-seat pricing models are under pressure. AI coding agents are helping companies rebuild tools internally. Venture investors are openly saying they’re no longer interested in “thin AI wrappers,” generic vertical SaaS, or surface-level workflow tools.

Capital is reallocating.

Not away from software.

Away from rented workflows.

---

## The Invisible Problem: You Don’t Own Your Process

For the last 15 years, SaaS won by locking humans into interfaces.

You paid per seat.  
Your team logged in.  
Work happened inside someone else’s system.

That felt safe.

But the real asset was never the interface.  
It was the workflow.

Now agents can execute tasks directly. They don’t care about logging into five dashboards. They don’t need seats. They don’t respect UI stickiness.

If an AI agent can:
- Pull the data
- Execute the action
- Deliver the output

The seat disappears.

And when the seat disappears, so does the pricing power.

---

## What This Means for Operators

If you’re running a service business, agency, consulting firm, or ops-heavy company, this shift is not abstract.

Look at your stack.

CRM.  
Project management.  
Reporting dashboards.  
Customer support systems.  
Internal trackers.

How many of those tools exist because humans need somewhere to “do the work”?

Now imagine:

Instead of three coordinators updating systems, an automated workflow ingests inputs, routes decisions, updates records, and pushes outputs - automatically.

No manual syncing.  
No status chasing.  
No swivel-chair operations.

That’s not a feature upgrade.

That’s structural margin expansion.

Or structural margin loss - if your competitor does it first.

---

## This Isn’t the Death of Software. It’s the Death of Surface Software.

The companies under pressure right now are the ones that live at the surface:

- UI-heavy layers
- Light automation
- Workflow visibility without workflow control
- Human-seat dependency

Investors are shifting toward companies that own:

Input -> Process -> Output

Not dashboards. Not logins. Not integrations.

Ownership.

Because agents can replicate features.

They cannot easily replicate embedded process knowledge, proprietary data loops, and deeply integrated workflows.

That’s the new moat.

---

## AI Is Becoming Infrastructure

Notice what’s happening:

Build vs. buy is flipping.  
Per-seat pricing is breaking.  
Outcome-based pricing is emerging.  
Companies are replacing teams with internal agents.

This isn’t experimentation.

This is infrastructure being rebuilt.

When infrastructure changes, everyone downstream feels it.

The question is simple:

Are you building your own workflow leverage -  
or renting someone else’s?

## Make It Concrete

Manual workflow:

Lead comes in -> human logs into CRM -> assigns task -> emails client -> updates project board -> follows up manually -> generates report.

Automated workflow:

Lead comes in -> system qualifies -> routes -> generates proposal -> schedules call -> updates CRM -> triggers onboarding -> creates reporting dashboard.

Human judgment still exists.

But machines execute.

That difference compounds daily.
