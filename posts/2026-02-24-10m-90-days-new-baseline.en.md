# $10M in 90 Days Is the New Baseline

Stripe just reported that **twice as many startups hit $10M ARR in three months in 2025 compared to 2024**.

And 20% of new companies charged their first customer within 30 days.

That’s not a product signal.

That’s a physics change.

---

## The Invisible Shift

In 2015, hitting $10M ARR in three to five years was impressive.

In 2024, founders still celebrated $10M in three years.

Now?

Ninety days.

The ramp didn’t improve.

It collapsed.

And this isn’t random.

The companies hitting these numbers aren’t building from scratch. They’re building on mature infrastructure:

- AI-native development cycles (code generation, rapid iteration, embedded copilots)
- API-first distribution (Stripe, AWS, Twilio, OpenAI - plug, don’t build)
- Agent-led execution (support, onboarding, research, outbound handled by software)
- No-code internal stacks replacing ops hires

They’re not moving faster because they’re smarter.

They’re moving faster because the engine underneath them is different.

Entire categories of friction are disappearing.

---

## From 3-Year Ramp to 90-Day Ramp

Old path:
- Raise capital
- Hire 15-30 people
- Build custom infrastructure
- Manually execute sales, ops, fulfillment
- Add managers to manage managers

New path:
- 3-5 operators
- API stack instead of internal builds
- Agents handling onboarding, QA, customer support, prospect research
- Revenue within weeks

Manual execution -> agent orchestration.
Headcount -> leverage.
Time -> compression.

When time compresses, capital compounds faster.

That’s the story.

## Show the Gears Turning

An AI-native startup can:

- Spin up infrastructure in a weekend.

- Use AI to generate product iterations daily.

And when the stack matures, velocity spikes.

Different physics.
